# Inbound Call Lead Funnel for Life Insurance

## Purpose

This document provides a **comprehensive, end-to-end blueprint** for building a **high-intent inbound call lead funnel** for life insurance. The goal is to create a **scalable, compliant, defensible acquisition asset** that converts callers into issued policies efficiently.

This is not a short-term lead-buying guide. It is an **ownership model**.

---

## 1. Strategic Foundation

### 1.1 Why Inbound Calls Dominate Life Insurance

Inbound calls outperform form leads because they:

- Capture **immediate intent**
- Self-select motivated buyers
- Reduce agent follow-up overhead
- Convert 2–5× higher than outbound or form-based leads
- Are resilient to iOS, cookie loss, and attribution decay

The funnel is designed to **create conversations, not clicks**.

---

### 1.2 Choose a Single Initial Market (Non-Negotiable)

Start with **one tightly defined market**:

- Final Expense (50–85)
- Guaranteed Issue
- Mortgage Protection (25–55)
- Veterans
- Small business / key person
- ACA crossover → term

Each market differs in:

- Compliance language
- Price sensitivity
- Call handling
- Underwriting constraints
- Close cycle length

**Generic messaging destroys inbound call quality.**

---

## 2. Funnel Architecture (System View)

```
Traffic Source
   ↓
Message Pre-Frame (Ad / Content)
   ↓
Call-Optimized Landing Page
   ↓
Tracked Phone Number (DNI)
   ↓
IVR / Call Routing
   ↓
Licensed Agent Conversation
   ↓
Quote → Application → Close
   ↓
CRM + Attribution + Retargeting
```

Every layer exists to **increase call quality and close probability**.

## 3. Traffic Acquisition Strategy

### 3.1 Channel Priority

#### Tier 1 – Highest Intent

**Google Search (Call Ads + Search Ads)**

- “life insurance quote phone number”
- “final expense insurance call now”
- “life insurance for veterans near me”

Characteristics:

- Lowest volume
- Highest intent
- Highest close rate

---

#### Tier 2 – Scalable Intent

**YouTube Video Ads**

- Educational framing
- Authority-based messaging
- Strong CTA to call

**Native Ads (Taboola / Outbrain)**

- Works well for senior markets
- Requires strong pre-frame

---

#### Tier 3 – Long-Term Asset

**SEO + Content**

- Comparison articles
- Eligibility explainers
- “What happens if…” scenarios
- Persistent click-to-call

---

### 3.2 Traffic Rules

- Never send cold traffic directly to a phone number
- Always pre-frame expectations
- Always disclose licensed agent status
- Optimize for **call duration**, not click-through rate

---

## 4. Call-Focused Landing Page Design

### 4.1 Page Objective

The landing page has **one job**:

> Make calling feel obvious, safe, and beneficial.

---

### 4.2 Above-the-Fold Structure

- Headline: Outcome-focused
- Subheadline: Expectation-setting
- Large phone number (tap-to-call)
- CTA button: “Call to Check Eligibility”

Example:

> See What Life Insurance You Actually Qualify For

---

### 4.3 Trust & Compliance Elements

- Licensed agent disclosure
- State license numbers
- Not affiliated with government disclaimer
- Carrier logos (where permitted)
- TCPA consent language
- Privacy policy

Trust increases **call initiation rate**.

---

### 4.4 Soft Pre-Qualification (Optional)

Before call:

- Age range
- State
- Smoker status

Benefits:

- Improves routing
- Filters unqualified callers
- Improves ad algorithm feedback

---

## 5. Call Infrastructure & Telephony

### 5.1 Call Tracking (Mandatory)

Track:

- Source (keyword, ad, campaign)
- Call duration
- Recording
- Outcome

Use:

- Dynamic Number Insertion (DNI)
- Keyword-level attribution

No tracking = no optimization.

---

### 5.2 IVR Best Practices

Keep IVR minimal:

- Recording disclosure
- Intent confirmation

Example:

> If you’re calling to get a life insurance quote, press 1.

Anything longer reduces conversions.

---

### 5.3 Call Routing Logic

Route calls by:

- State licensing
- Product specialization
- Age band
- Language
- New vs return caller

Bad routing kills close rates.

---

## 6. Agent Call Framework

### 6.1 First 90 Seconds (Critical)

Objectives:

- Confirm intent
- Reduce anxiety
- Establish authority
- Keep prospect talking

Position agent as **advisor**, not salesperson.

---

### 6.2 Discovery Framework

Collect:

- Age
- Health overview
- Budget sensitivity
- Coverage purpose

Avoid early product pushing.

---

### 6.3 Quote Presentation

Never lead with price.

Instead:

- Explain policy categories
- Explain qualification logic
- Present options, not a single quote

This prevents price-only decisions.

---

## 7. Compliance & Risk Management

### 7.1 TCPA & Recording

- Verbal recording disclosure
- Written consent on page
- Clear opt-out mechanisms

---

### 7.2 Advertising Compliance

Avoid:

- Guaranteed approval claims
- False urgency
- Income or benefit exaggeration

---

### 7.3 Carrier Compliance

- Approved scripts
- Product-specific disclosures
- State-specific language

Compliance failure can shut down the funnel entirely.

---

## 8. CRM, Attribution & Follow-Up

### 8.1 CRM Requirements

CRM must:

- Auto-log calls
- Store recordings
- Capture source attribution
- Track pipeline outcomes

Inbound funnels require feedback loops.

---

### 8.2 Missed Call Recovery

Missed calls = lost revenue.

Implement:

- Instant SMS follow-up
- Automatic callback within 60 seconds
- Agent alerts

---

### 8.3 Retargeting Strategy

Retarget:

- Page visitors
- Short calls
- Abandoned calls

Messaging:

- “Still have questions?”
- “Rates vary by health — check yours.”
